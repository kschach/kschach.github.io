Methodology Chapter Outline

August 24, 2018

Methodology

Research Design (or Research Framework)

  • Importance of ontology, epistemology, axiology, and methodology in research design (Creswell 2013)
  • Applied Critical Realism offers a cohesive approach (Edwards, O’Mahoney, and Vincent 2014; Fletcher 2013, 2017)

Applied Critical Realism

  • Distinguish from constructivism and positivism
  • Three layer ontology – empirical, actual, real
  • Ontology > epistemology
  • Distinct modes of analysis – description, abduction, retroduction
  • Goal to explain and identify causal mechanisms
  • Explanation rather than thick description or hypothesis testing
  • Intensive and extensive methods
  • Sources:
    • Fletcher (2013)
    • Fletcher (2017)
    • Edwards et al. (2014)
    • Easton (2010)
    • Canonical texts in CR?
      • Collier (1994)
      • Sayer (1992)
      • Maxwell (2012)

Justification for Using Applied CR

  • As a means to explicate theory of change (what changes for whom and why) and identify causal mechanisms
  • Accounts for structure and agency
  • Useful to develop a theory explaining the existence of nonprofit access to benefits programs, the limitations on their agency, and identify causal mechanisms for and resulting from their existence (what causes their existence AND what effects do they have)

Comparative Case Study Approach

  • Between historical movements to increase access to welfare state income benefits and contemporary nonprofit access to benefits programs.
  • Between local, contemporary access to benefits programs to identify the range of program models
  • Identify and analyze the results of each case (redistribution, punitive measures), the interventions utilized, agents involved, structural influences, and causal mechanisms (barriers to / resources for agency towards redistribution).
  • Sources:
    • Edwards et al. (2014)

Data Analysis

  • Goal to explain what is happening in the field and the relationship between ongoing practice and socioeconomic justice
  • Analyze transcripts and documents with an emphasis on uncovering causal mechanisms that can explain the field being studied
  • Used RQDA software for computer-assisted qualitative data analysis
  • Why RQDA
  • Sources:
    • Huang (2016)
    • Chandra and Shang (2017)
    • Varner et al. (2012)
    • Estrada (2017)

Coding

  • Provisional coding – deductive codes from theory and literature review
  • Negative case analysis – to not allow deductive codes to overdetermine analysis
  • CR coding – identify structure and agency
  • Per case coding – to ensure connection between participants and their responses, to avoid overabstraction
  • Results of coding: number of codes in each phase; types of codes; how the codes changed through the iterative process
  • Sources:
    • c.f. Fletcher (2013)
    • Fletcher (2017)
    • Gilgun (2014)
